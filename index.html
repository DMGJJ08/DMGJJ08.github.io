Spotify Music Popularity Analysis

A music label wants to know which types of songs to invest in next. Using 114,000 Spotify tracks, I identified the genres, energy levels, and artist profiles that drive the highest popularity.

// The Problem
What are we solving?
Music labels invest millions in artists and genres. Without data, these are gut decisions. What actually makes a song popular on Spotify?
// The Analysis
What did I do?
Analyzed 114,000 tracks across genre, energy, danceability, and artist data. Cleaned duplicates, ran SQL queries, and built visualizations to compare popularity scores.
// The Findings
What did I find?
Pop-film and K-pop dominate. High-energy tracks consistently outperform. Latin artists are rising fast. Explicit tracks slightly outperform clean ones.

Healthcare Patient Readmission Analysis

Hospitals face heavy financial penalties for patients readmitted within 30 days. Using 99,493 patient records, I identified which patient profiles are at highest risk — giving hospitals a data-backed way to prioritize follow-up care.

// The Problem
What are we solving?
Hospitals are penalized millions for 30-day readmissions. Which patients are most likely to return? Knowing this lets hospitals intervene before discharge.
// The Analysis
What did I do?
Cleaned 99,493 patient records, removed missing values, analyzed age groups, medication counts, hospital stay length, and primary diagnoses against readmission rates.
// The Findings
What did I find?
Ages 20-30 have the highest readmission rate at 14%. Heart failure patients dominate readmissions. Short stays of 2-3 days are deceptively risky.

// Business Recommendation
Based on this analysis, the hospital should:
  • Flag heart failure patients (Code 428) as high priority for mandatory follow-up within 7 days of discharge.
  • Create a high-risk monitoring program for patients aged 20-30 — their 14% readmission rate is the highest across all age groups.
  • Do not discharge patients after only 2-3 days without a structured post-discharge care plan.
  • Patients prescribed 15+ medications should receive a medication review before discharge to reduce complexity-driven readmissions.
