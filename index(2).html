Spotify Music Popularity Analysis

A music label wants to know which types of songs to invest in next. Using 114,000 Spotify tracks, I identified the genres, energy levels, and artist profiles that drive the highest popularity — giving a data-backed answer on where to focus releases.

// The Problem
What are we solving?
Music labels invest millions in artists and genres. Without data, these are gut decisions. The question: what actually makes a song popular on Spotify? Answering this helps labels invest smarter.
// The Analysis
What did I do?
Analyzed 114,000 tracks across genre, energy, danceability, and artist data. Cleaned duplicates, ran SQL queries, and built visualizations to compare popularity scores across every variable.
// The Findings
What did I find?
Pop-film and K-pop dominate popularity. High-energy tracks consistently outperform low-energy ones. Latin artists are rising fast — Bad Bunny appears twice in the top 10. Explicit tracks slightly outperform clean ones.

// Business Recommendation
Based on this analysis, a music label should:
  • Prioritize pop-film and K-pop genre releases — they consistently score highest in platform popularity.
  • Invest in high-energy productions — tracks with energy scores above 0.6 significantly outperform low-energy alternatives.
  • Actively pursue Latin artist collaborations — Bad Bunny, Manuel Turizo, and Bizarrap dominate the top popularity tier.
  • Don't avoid explicit content — explicit tracks perform better on the platform and should not be restricted without cause.
